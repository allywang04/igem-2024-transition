The Problem

As of now, the development of a phage therapy involves hundreds of repetitions of a scientist preparing wet lab procedure, preparing reagents, culturing an antibiotic resistant bacteria, isolating phage, co-incubating the phage and bacteria, and measuring the phage’s parasitization of the bacteria. With each step being prone to failure, the entire process is highly costly and takes months to complete.
